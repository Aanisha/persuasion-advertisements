Introduction

Marketing communications are the means by which companies and governments inform, remind and persuade their consumers (including citizens) about the products and brands they sell. They are the primary means of connecting brand with consumers through which the consumer can know what the product is about, what it stands for, who makes it and can be motivated to try it out. Marketing communication by conveying meaning allows marketers to link their brands with people, places, experiences, feelings, events, and other things. To introduce meaning into their communication, marketers use rhetorical devices such as symbolism (a dove picture as a peace symbol), slogans (Oreo's "Celebrate the Kid Inside"), emotions (liking by showing a person enjoying after having Coke, humor by showing Ronald McDonald visiting a Burger King store), reasoning ("One glass of Florida orange juice contains 75% of your daily vitamin C needs"), and social identity and impact (Airbnb showing a mother with her child and the headline "My home is funding her future").
